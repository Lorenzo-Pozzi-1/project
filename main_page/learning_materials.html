🧪 Environmental Impact Quotient (EIQ).

EIQ is a numerical score that quantifies the total environmental impact of a pesticide application. Developed at Cornell University, it considers worker safety, consumer exposure, and ecological effects in a single value.

What EIQ Measures

Component Risk Category
Farmworker Impact Applicator and field worker exposure risk
Consumer Impact Food residue risk to end consumers
Ecological Impact Risk to non-target species and environment

How EIQ is Calculated

Field Use EIQ = (Base EIQ × Application Rate) / (LD50 × 1000)

Base EIQ is calculated from 11 toxicological and environmental fate parameters including chronic toxicity, persistence, leaching potential, and toxicity to fish, birds, and beneficial insects.

Score Interpretation

Field Use EIQ Environmental Impact Typical Potato Applications
< 20 Lower impact Copper fungicides, biologicals
20-50 Moderate impact Modern fungicides, selective insecticides
50-100 Higher impact Broad-spectrum insecticides
> 100 Very high impact Older chemistry, high rates

💡 Key Points for Potato Production

  • Compare products within the same category (fungicide to fungicide)
  • Consider application frequency—6 sprays = 6x seasonal impact
  • Lower application rates can offset higher base EIQ values
  • Set seasonal EIQ targets for your operation (e.g., < 200 total)
  • EIQ is comparative, not an absolute safety measure

🌊 Surface Water Tool for Pesticide Risk (STIR).

STIR is a USDA risk assessment model that specifically evaluates pesticide contamination potential to surface water through runoff and drift. It focuses exclusively on aquatic ecosystem protection.

What STIR Measures

Risk Factor Assessment Focus
Runoff Potential Surface water contamination via field runoff
Leaching Potential Groundwater to surface water movement
Aquatic Toxicity Direct harm to fish and aquatic organisms
Environmental Persistence Duration of active contamination risk

How STIR is Calculated

STIR = Predicted Environmental Concentration / Aquatic Toxicity Threshold

PEC considers application rate, solubility, soil binding, persistence, and volatility. Toxicity threshold is based on effects to fish, invertebrates, and aquatic plants.

Risk Level Interpretation

STIR Value Risk Level Management Response
< 0.1 Low risk Standard practices acceptable
0.1 - 1.0 Moderate risk Consider timing, buffers
1.0 - 10 High risk Implement risk mitigation
> 10 Very high risk Consider alternatives

⚠️ Key Points for Potato Production

  • Critical for fields near streams, rivers, or wetlands
  • Avoid high-STIR applications before rainfall (>10mm in 24hr)
  • Use vegetated buffer zones to reduce runoff risk
  • STIR values influence EPA registration decisions
  • Site-specific conditions (slope, soil) affect actual risk
  • Prioritize low-STIR products for sensitive locations

EIQ vs STIR: When to Use Each

Use EIQ When... Use STIR When...
Overall sustainability assessment Fields near water bodies
Comparing broad environmental impact Regulatory compliance focus
Season-long program optimization Site-specific risk management
Certification requirements Aquatic habitat protection
